SUMMARY

A GTP cyclohydrolase II-encoding gene from Arabidopsis thaliana was isolated through functional complementation of a mutant of Escherichia coli, BSV18, deficient in this protein. The derived amino-acid sequence constitutes a polypeptide of 27 kDa and shows 37-58% identity with previously published sequences of Escherichia coli, Bacillus subtilis, Photobacterium leiognathi and P. phosphoreum.

GTP cyclohydrolase II (GCH; EC 3.5.4.20) is the first enzyme of riboflavin (vitamin B2) biosynthesis, catalyzing the release of formate and PPi under formation of 2,5-diamino-6-ribosylamino-4(3H)-pyrimidine 5'-phosphate from GTP (Foor and Brown, 1975). Although the enzyme has been purified from microorganisms, and the gene encoding it isolated and characterized, very little is known about plant GCH. To understand riboflavin synthesis in plants, we isolated cDNAs encoding GCH from the higher plant Arabidopsis thaliana (At), functional complementation of an E. coli strain, BSV18, defective in this enzyme (Bandrin et al., 1983). While the identity of the start Met is not known, the sequence surrounding the first ATG codon (GGTTCCATGGC), starting at nt 61, conforms to the close consensus sequence for the start Met in eukaryotes (Kozak, 1991). The 3'-UTR consists of 387 nt, with a putative polyadenylation signal of AATAAA 17 nt upstream from the poly(A) tail. Computer-aided comparisons show that the aa sequence of At GCH is 58, 47, 37 and 39% identical with that of E. coli (Richter et al., 1993), B. subtilis (Kil et al., 1992), P. leiognathi (Lee and Meighen, 1992) and P. phosphoreum (Lee et al., 1994). The arrangement of the GCH gene(s) in the genome of At was explored by a Southern blot of total At (var. Landberg erecta) DNA digested with BamHI, EcoRI and XhoI, using complete cDNA as a probe. The digests showed single bands (BamHI, 12 kb; EcoRI, 4.4 kb; XhoI, 15 kb). It is likely that there is a single copy of the gene in At. Riboflavin is a water-soluble vitamin whose important coenzyme forms are riboflavin Y-phosphate and flavin adenine dinucleotide. Riboflavin is one of the candidates for the photoreceptor substance for plants blue-lightdependent processes (Ahmad and Cashmore, 1993).
