X-ray intensity data to 1.8 A resolution have been collected from a single crystal of benzamidlne-inhibited bovine fl-trypsin at pH 7.0. Given the refined atomic co-ordinates as found in the crystal structure of bovine trypsin-pancreatic trypsin inhibitor complex (Huber et al., 1974) the trypsin molecules have been located within the trypsin crystals applying Patterson search techniques. Using the optimal orientation and position parameters a crystallographic _~-factor of 0.43 was calculated for data from 6.8 to 1.8 A resolution.

1. Introduction
Rfihlmann d al. (1973) have published the structure of the complex formed by bovine fl-trypsin and bovine pancreatic trypsin inhibitor. Recently Huber e t a / . (1974) determined the refined co-ordinates of the complex at 1'.9 A resolution obtained by constrained crystallographic refinement (Deisenhofer & Steigemann, 1974,1975). An important question concerning this complex structure is where and to what extent structural changes within both complex components occur on binding. While a highly refined structure of the pancreatic trypsin inhibitor has been presented b y Deisenhofer & Steigemann (1975), until now no detailed comparison has been possible for the trypsin component due to lack of reliable high-resohition model data. As already mentioned by Huber d al. (1974) and shown in the accompanying paper deallng with the refined fl-trypsin structure (Bode & Schwager, 1975) the modeis of inhibited bovine trypsin recently published by Stroud et a~.
